Zhuang Zi Speaks: The Music of Nature

July 8, 2023

Zhuang Zi/Chuang-Tzu/Zhuangzi/莊子 was an influential writer and philosopher who discussed many topics that both countered and supported Buddhist principles. He was a native of the state of Meng (now located in present-day Henan province) and was born during the Warring States period in China. His ideas focused not only on the characteristics of the Dao, but also the role of nature and human existence. 

Origin

Henan province, China

Chinese Through Comics Series excerpts

Chinese philosophy has long been studied by Western scholars, but even the smallest error or misinterpretation of the translation can affect the original meaning of the author. Zuang Zi's writing was a product of his time and Western interpretations and readings are heavily influenced by Western philosophy. Interpretations from a Chinese writer, on the other hand, can at least provide a fair reading of Zuang Zi's philosophy.
